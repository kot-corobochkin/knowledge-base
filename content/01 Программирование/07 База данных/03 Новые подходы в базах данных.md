## 3. Current Database Landscape

**Alive and well:**
- **Search databases** (Elasticsearch, now being rewritten in Rust; new players like Ceredb).
- **Vector databases** (driven by AI/RAG), though most SQL databases are adding vector support.
- **Columnar databases** (ClickHouse)—a real breakthrough for analytics, enabling fast scans of large datasets.
- **PostgreSQL**—the default choice for most new projects.

**Niche but surviving:**
- **Tarantool**—an in‑memory database with Lua scripting (bring your code close to data). Still used in high‑frequency trading, exchanges, and some Russian infrastructure.
- **Firebird**—still used in industrial/embedded systems (e.g., cash registers, Abrams tanks) due to reliability and autotomy.
- **Time‑series databases** (InfluxDB) have been largely absorbed by columnar databases like ClickHouse.

**What’s dead or shrinking:**
- The idea of **separate JSON/document databases**—almost all SQL databases now have JSON support.
- The boom of **NoSQL as a replacement for SQL**—it never materialized.
