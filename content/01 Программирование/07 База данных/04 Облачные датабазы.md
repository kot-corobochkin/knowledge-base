## 5. Cloud Databases and Vendor Lock‑in
- Cloud databases (Amazon RDS, DynamoDB, Neon, Turso, etc.) are growing, but vendor lock‑in remains a concern.
- **DynamoDB** has become a quasi‑standard (S3‑like API), but specialized cloud databases often remain niche.
- The **real cloud revolution** is that large‑scale shared‑nothing databases (like DynamoDB) are moving into proprietary cloud offerings, while open‑source databases focus on single‑node performance.

Vendor lock-in означает ты становишься зависим от **конкретного облака**.

Например, если ты используешь:

- Amazon Web Services
- и их сервис Amazon RDS
- или Amazon DynamoDB

то потом уйти на другое облако очень сложно.
