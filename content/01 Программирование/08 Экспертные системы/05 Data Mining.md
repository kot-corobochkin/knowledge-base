## 10. Data Mining

Data mining is the computational process of discovering patterns in large datasets.
- **Goal:** To extract information from a dataset and transform it into an understandable structure for further use.
- **Process:** It analyzes data, often stored in data warehouses, to interpret patterns through real-time analysis.
- **Applications:** Used by managers to decide on marketing strategies, increase sales, and compare products with competitors.

## 11. Big Data Mining

Big data mining deals with the analysis of extremely large and complex datasets generated by modern technology.
- **Data Sources:** Sensors, automated processes, multimedia, cameras, satellites, mobile phones, and user activities on the Internet.
- **Growth Drivers:** The proliferation of cloud computing, wireless networks, and the Internet, where every user activity is recorded.
- **Purpose:** To analyze patterns and trends to aid in decision-making across various fields, including business, healthcare, finance, and scientific research.
- **Key Players:** Companies like Google, Amazon, Twitter, and Facebook were among the first to develop solutions for handling the exponential growth of data.
