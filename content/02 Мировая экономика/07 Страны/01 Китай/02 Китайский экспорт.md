## 1. Three Aspects of China’s Trade Dominance

The authors identify three defining features of China's current trade position:

| Feature | Description |
| --- | --- |
| Broad-based export expansion | China gained global export market share in nearly all manufacturing sectors over the past decade—from low-value goods (apparel, textiles) to advanced products (automobiles, electronics). Contrary to expectations, China has moved up the value chain without ceding share in lower-value industries. |
| Declining market shares for advanced economies | Exporters in Japan, the euro area (especially Germany), and other advanced economies have experienced widespread losses across most sectors, reflecting how China's rise is reshaping global manufacturing competitiveness. |
| Asymmetric import growth | Chinese imports have lagged behind exports and become concentrated in commodities and commodity-intensive inputs (now 44% of total imports). Imports of manufactured goods from advanced economies have grown slowly, shifting the geography of China's growth spillovers toward commodity-exporting economies (e.g., Australia, Brazil, Latin America). |
