## 2.1. Huliaipole Sector (Zaporizhzhia Region)

Russian forces captured a tactical area of just over **2.5 km²** south of Huliaipole, pushing towards Zaliznychne. This was achieved after more than a week of continuous assaults.

## 2.2. Russian Strategic Intent

The goal is to create a buffer of control around Huliaipole and Zaliznychne, potentially to **secure railway lines** for simplified logistics in a future offensive towards Zaporizhzhia.

## 2.3. Operational Dynamics

While the Russian **5th Combined Arms Army** is attempting this advance, the **29th and 36th armies** to the north are significantly weakened by Ukrainian counterattacks. Ukraine is systematically pushing Russian forces back from the administrative border of the Dnipropetrovsk region, conducting "clearing" operations. Russia is concentrating its remaining striking power on one axis while losing ground on its northern flank.

## 3. Southern Donbas: The Main Axis of Conflict

The **Southern Donbas** (Huliaipole sector) has become the focal point of both Ukrainian and Russian efforts.

## 3.1. Ukrainian Operations
- Ukrainian forces are pushing **from the north** (Dnipropetrovsk region border) into Zaporizhzhia Oblast.
- They have already entered Zaporizhzhia territory along the **left bank of the Haychul River** and are advancing toward the Yanchuk River.
- This creates a **critical situation for Russia**, which is struggling to hold the administrative boundary.

## 3.2. Russian Operations
- Russia is attempting to advance **from the south** through Huliaipole, with the primary goal of **capturing the railway** to secure logistics for a future offensive toward Zaporizhzhia.
- However, Russia is simultaneously **losing control on the northern flank and the left bank** of the Haychul River.
- The Russian grouping in this sector is **exhausted**. The 5th Combined Arms Army, which is the main striking force, has already been weakened and received reinforcements taken from other units—a classic sign of degradation rather than genuine reserve deployment.

| --- | --- | --- |
| Southern | From Huliaipole along the Haychul River | Pressure toward Zaporizhzhia |
| Eastern | From Donetsk Oblast into Dnipropetrovshchyna | Outflank Ukrainian defenses from the east |

However, this strategy is already **failing**:
- The Russian campaign in Zaporizhzhia and Dnipropetrovsk regions is **already lost**.
- Any further Russian efforts will likely follow the pattern of **"one step forward, two steps back"** —a dynamic that has become characteristic of the front line.
- Russian forces lack fresh reserves; they are merely redistributing exhausted units.
