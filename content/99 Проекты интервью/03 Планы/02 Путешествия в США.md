# 4-Week Preparation Plan Before Your Visa Interview

## Week 1 — Core documents (foundation)

Goal: make sure the most important documents are ready.

**Tasks**

- Check that your **DS-160 confirmation page** is saved and printed.
- Print the **interview appointment confirmation**.
- Check your **passport validity** (at least 6 months after the planned trip).
- Organize your **invitation letter from the conference**.
- Save:
    - conference website
    - conference program
    - proof of registration (if you have it).
- Create one folder (digital + printed) for all documents.

**Also useful this week**

- Look at flight routes (don’t rush buying yet).
- Check hotel options near the conference.

---

## Week 2 — Supporting documents

Goal: strengthen your case for the interview.

**Prepare**

- Proof of work:
    - employment letter or contract
    - short description of your role.
- Financial documents:
    - bank statement
    - or proof that the trip is funded.
- Connection to your home country:
    - work contract
    - projects
    - property / rental agreement (if available).
- If you present at the conference:
    - confirmation of talk
    - title of presentation.

**Important step**  
Make a **simple explanation of your trip** (1–2 sentences).

Example:

> I am attending a conference in Chicago from June X to June X.

---

## Week 3 — Logistics and risk reduction

Goal: remove surprises.

**Tasks**

- Decide the flight route you will buy **after visa approval**.
- Check transit rules for the route you plan.
- Prepare delivery plan for passport from Kazakhstan.
- Recheck:
    - DS-160 number matches the appointment
    - passport details are correct.
- Prepare printed copies of everything.

Create this folder structure:

Interview folder:

- passport
- DS-160 confirmation
- appointment confirmation
- invitation letter
- work documents
- bank statement
- conference info

---

## Week 4 — Final preparation

Goal: be fully ready and calm before travel.

**Tasks**

- Print all documents again (backup set).
- Prepare answers to common questions:
    - Why are you going to the US?
    - What is the conference?
    - Who pays for the trip?
    - What do you do for work?
    - How long will you stay?
- Book hotel in the interview city if needed.
- Plan travel to the embassy location.
- Prepare a simple interview explanation about your trip.

**2–3 days before interview**

- Check documents one last time.
- Put everything in one folder.
- Sleep well (this actually matters).

---

# A small but very useful trick

Most successful interviews follow this pattern:

**clear purpose + clear return home**

So your story should be simple:

- conference
- short visit
- return after event
