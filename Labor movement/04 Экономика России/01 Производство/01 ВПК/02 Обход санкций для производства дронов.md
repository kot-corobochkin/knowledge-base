## 2. Financial and Logistical Evasion Tactics

Chinese suppliers employ several methods to bypass sanctions:
- **Closed-Loop Financial Architecture:** Payments are routed through Russia's VTB Bank, allowing settlement in national currencies without using the SWIFT network.
- **Third-Country Transshipment:** Components are delivered via Kyrgyzstan, Turkey, and other intermediate nations to avoid direct Russia-China shipping scrutiny.
- **Cryptocurrency:** One supplier requested payment in USDT (Tether), a dollar-pegged cryptocurrency enabling rapid transfers outside traditional banking systems.

## 4. Key Findings
- **Systematic Circumvention:** Chinese companies knowingly sell military-grade components to Russia despite Beijing's 2023 export restrictions.
- **Sophisticated Evasion:** Suppliers use VTB Bank accounts, cryptocurrency, third-country transshipment, and misdeclared customs forms to avoid detection.
- **Price Exploitation:** Russian buyers face inflated prices (2.5–4× normal rates) due to surging demand and the destruction of domestic Russian production capacity.
- **Dual-Use Supply Chains:** Some suppliers (e.g., Ubeesky) appear to be arming both sides of the conflict, marketing countermeasures to Russian systems they also help supply.
- **Obfuscation in Trade Data:** Many firms identified in undercover communications do not appear in Russian customs databases, suggesting the use of shell companies or redacted records.
