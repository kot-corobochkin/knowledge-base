## 4. Europe’s Illusion of Security

## 4.1. Air Defense Vulnerabilities

In 2025, Poland detected Russian Gerbera decoy drones (costing around $10,000) entering its airspace. To shoot them down, Poland used **AIM-120 AMRAAM missiles**, costing over **$1 million each**.

As Kovalenko notes, Poland was not prepared to use elementary short-range air defense systems that have long existed in Ukraine: mobile fire groups, cheap missiles, combat crews. He asks: "What will happen if these are aeroballistic missiles? Kinzhals? Tsirkons?"

## 4.2. No Interior Defense Lines

Russia has not conducted mass tank attacks for over a year; its war has shifted to mobile infantry actions under the cover of artillery and drones. This experience could be used against EU member states. Yet Europe lacks **prepared interior defense lines**—there are no deeply fortified positions or pre-positioned ATGM emplacements along borders in Lithuania, Latvia, Estonia, or Poland.

This gap creates space for genuine Polish-Ukrainian cooperation: **Poland has a powerful defense industrial base; Ukraine has unprecedented practical experience of modern warfare.**
