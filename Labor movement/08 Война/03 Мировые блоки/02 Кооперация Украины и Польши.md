
## 1. The Nature of Polish-Ukrainian Defense Cooperation

The article argues that Polish-Ukrainian defense cooperation is not based on charity but on **mutually beneficial defense agreements**.
- **T-72 Tanks:** Poland’s transfer of approximately 200 T-72 tanks in the first months of the full-scale invasion was both a gesture of solidarity and a pragmatic calculation. Polish military analysis as early as 2021 (during Dragon-21 exercises) had already considered the possibility of a Russian attack from Belarus and the need to withdraw to the Vistula line. Soviet-era equipment was viewed as potential wartime losses. By transferring it to Ukraine, Poland simultaneously strengthened the Ukrainian front, accelerated its own modernization, and freed resources for new procurement (Abrams, K2, modern air defense systems).
- **Beyond Free Aid:** A significant portion of Polish military aid has been acquired through **commercial contracts**:

Krab self-propelled howitzers — contract.
- Rosomak wheeled armored vehicles — contract.
- At least **700 drones** transferred since the start of the full-scale war.
- Loitering munitions purchased by Ukraine from Poland even before 2022.

This marks a shift from a "donor-recipient" model toward the gradual formation of a **shared defense space**.

## 5. Joint Production and Industrial Security

## 5.1. Relocating Production to Poland

Military analyst Pavlo Narozhnyi raises the possibility of relocating part of Ukraine’s defense production to Poland.

Ukraine remains under constant missile and drone attack. A recent strike on the Flamingo missile production line (publicly confirmed by the President of Ukraine) highlighted the vulnerability of the production cycle. Poland, by contrast, offers a more manageable security environment.

Already, a significant portion of Western equipment is being repaired in Poland. The next step is **joint production**.

## 5.2. The Bohdana Howitzer in Poland

According to Business Insider Polska (citing Puls Biznesu), the Polish-Ukrainian company **PK MIL SA** is preparing to launch production of the **Bohdana self-propelled howitzer** in Poland.
- **Bohdana** is a modern Ukrainian 155mm artillery system, battle-proven in Ukraine.
- The joint venture was established in October of the previous year between Polish company Ponar Wadowice and the Kramatorsk Heavy Machine Tool Building Plant.
- The company is finalizing formal procedures; 51% of shares are owned by the Polish side.
- With production in Poland, the Bohdana could also be acquired by the Polish Armed Forces.

## 5.3. MiG-29 Transfers

Poland has planned to transfer **9–10 MiG-29 fighter jets**, which will directly strengthen Ukraine’s defense.

The MiG-29 serves a dual function:
- **Interception:** effective against cruise missiles (Kh-101, Kalibr, Iskander-K) as a mobile air defense element.
- **Strike:** adapted to use Western-guided bombs (e.g., French AASM HAMMER), allowing high-precision strikes without entering dense air defense zones. The aircraft functions as both a shield and a sword.
