## 2.2. NATO Training Lags Behind Reality

A telling example occurred during the **Hedgehog 2025 exercises in Estonia**, involving over 16,000 troops from 12 NATO countries.
- A Ukrainian team of about **10 drone operators**, using the Delta situational awareness system, **simulated destroying 17 armored vehicles and conducting 30 strikes in less than half a day**.
- Over the course of a day, the Ukrainian-Estonian group effectively **knocked out two battalions**, paralyzing their operations.
- Estonian military officials called the result a **"shock"**, demonstrating that the modern battlefield is completely transparent and any movement is an instant target.

The conclusion: NATO tactics are still based on outdated methodologies that do not adequately account for FPV systems and drone warfare.

## 6. JATEC and Institutional Integration into NATO

Ukraine is not only receiving support but also **shaping new NATO collective defense standards** based on real wartime experience.

In February 2025, the **Joint Analysis, Training, and Education Centre (JATEC)** began operations in Bydgoszcz, Poland.
- It is the **first joint civil-military body** in NATO dedicated to analyzing Russia’s war against Ukraine and translating that experience into changes in planning and training.
- JATEC serves as a channel through which Ukrainian combat experience enters NATO’s internal system.

Ukrainian companies are already contracting with NATO’s Allied Command Transformation. Ukraine is exporting expertise, for example, through the **Delta situational awareness system**, which has been used as a tool for managing joint forces.

Ukrainian officers have been allowed to participate in **Article 5 exercises** (LOYAL DOLOS 25) and for the first time led opposition forces (OPFOR) during REPMUS/Dynamic Messenger 25. NATO is now training based on **real Ukrainian experience**.
