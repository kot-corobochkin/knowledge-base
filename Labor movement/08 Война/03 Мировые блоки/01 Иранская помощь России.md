## 3. Technological Exchange and the Evolution of the Shahed-136

Russia did not simply use Iranian drones—it **improved them** and engaged in a **two-way technology exchange** with Iran:

| Russian Modifications to Shahed-136 |
| --- |
| Addition of GLONASS navigation modules |
| Upgraded fuel systems for low-temperature operation |
| Russian B-101 flight control units and "Kometa" navigation modules |
| Redesigned airframe (honeycomb structure for durability) |
| Experimental warheads (thermobaric, incendiary, tandem, varying weights from 40 to 90 kg) |

**Key Point:** Russia and Iran exchanged improvements. Some drones shot down in Ukraine were Iranian-produced, while others were Russian-made "Geran-2" variants. Over the past year, Russia used **more than 30 modifications** of the Shahed-136 against Ukraine.

## 4. How Strikes on Iran Affect Russia

The strikes on Iran impact Russian military capabilities in three ways:
- **Disruption of Production:** Destruction of Shahed-136 manufacturing facilities and depletion of Iranian stockpiles.
- **Loss of a Key Supplier:** Iran was a strategic partner for both finished weapons and critical components.
- **Cutting Technological Exchange:** The bilateral flow of improvements and innovations has been interrupted.
