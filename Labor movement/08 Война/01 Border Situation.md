**1. Border Situation & "Grey Zone" Concept**

- The Ukrainian border with Russia, particularly in the Sumy, Chernihiv, and Kharkiv regions, is characterized by a "grey zone" extending 1-3 km into Ukrainian territory.
    
- This zone lacks permanent fortifications due to constant shelling, making it vulnerable.
    
- Villages located 50-500 meters from the border are effectively in this grey zone and under constant threat.
    
- Russian tactics involve small assault groups infiltrating these villages (e.g., Pokrovka, Grabovske, Sopych) rather than large-scale tank breakthroughs.
    
- Once Russian forces advance beyond this grey zone (approximately 3 km), they encounter established Ukrainian defensive lines and are typically stopped.
    

**2. Territorial Gains & Losses**

- In the first week of March, Russian forces captured approximately 41 km² of Ukrainian territory.
    
- However, this figure is offset by Ukrainian counterattacks, resulting in a net gain for Russia of about 12 km² for the week.
    
- Over 25 of the 41 km² captured were from the "grey zone" along the border, where resistance was minimal.
    

**3. Strategic Context**

- Russia is struggling to make significant gains along the main front lines, where they suffer extremely high casualties—over 200 personnel per km² captured.
    
- The incursions along the border serve three primary purposes:
    
    - **Propaganda:** To create a narrative of progress and open new fronts for their domestic audience.
        
    - **Distraction:** To stretch and divert the attention of Ukrainian defense forces.
        
    - **Hostage-Taking:** To capture Ukrainian civilians to use as bargaining chips for future prisoner exchanges.

## 3. The Border Incursions: Purpose and Limitations

Russian activity along the border (especially in Sumy Oblast) serves two purposes:
- **Information operations** — creating a narrative of continued advances for domestic consumption
- **Hostage-taking** — capturing Ukrainian civilians for future prisoner exchanges

However, these incursions are limited to the **grey zone** (1–3 km from the border). Russia lacks the resources for deeper penetrations of 10–20 km along the entire border. These are localized actions by small groups, not a strategic breakthrough.

**Key takeaway:** These border actions are tactical noise. The main events occur on the actual front line, where Russian forces face stagnation and degradation.
