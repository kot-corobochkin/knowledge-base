## 1. Background: The "Khyshnyk" Buggy

The **"Khyshnyk" (Predator)** buggy began its service in Ukrainian defense units in 2022. It started as a **volunteer initiative by enthusiasts in Vinnytsia** and has since become one of the most sought-after vehicles for operations near the line of contact.

## 2. Purpose and Role

The author emphasizes that the buggy is **not an assault vehicle**. Its intended roles are:
- **Logistics** (supply transport)
- **Evacuation** (casualty and equipment extraction)
- At most, **diversionary or raiding activities**

This distinction is important to avoid uninformed criticism from those who misunderstand its purpose.

## 3. Technical Evolution and Features

Over time, the "Khyshnyk" has undergone multiple improvements in both design and manufacturing processes:

| Feature | Details |
| --- | --- |
| Drive | Currently produced exclusively in all-wheel drive versions |
| Weight | Just over 1 ton |
| Frame | Reinforced structure for greater durability |
| Speed | Over 120 km/h on rough terrain |
| Terrain capability | Can climb steep slopes; superior off-road performance compared to many pickup trucks |
| Turret | Equipped with a 360° rotating turret with minimal blind spots—suitable for mobile fire groups |

## 3.1. Unique Production Detail

Some components are manufactured in-house using materials salvaged from **irreparable Russian captured equipment**, specifically **ABT-102 aluminum alloy**. The author notes this as symbolic.

## 4. Feedback from the 35th Marine Brigade

The 35th Marine Brigade reports that units using the "Khyshnyk" for logistics and other tasks have **minimized personnel losses** due to the vehicle's mobility and maneuverability.

## 4.1. Illustrative Incident

During a mission to deliver ammunition to a tank:
- A **Mitsubishi L-200 pickup** became stuck in difficult terrain while carrying **500 kg of ammunition**.
- A **"Khyshnyk" buggy**, also carrying **500 kg of ammunition**, followed the same route.
- The buggy not only navigated the terrain successfully but also **transferred the ammunition from the stuck L-200** and delivered it to the tank.
- On the return trip, it helped **extract both the L-200 and a Jeep** that had also gotten stuck trying to assist.

## 5. The "People’s Buggy"

The author describes the "Khyshnyk" as a **"people's buggy"** because its production has relied on:
- **Volunteer support**
- **Regular fundraising** for units that urgently need such vehicles

## 5.1. Market Position

Currently in Ukraine, the "Khyshnyk" has **almost no competitors** in its **price-to-quality category**. The author states it has become the best project in its class, difficult even for Western equivalents to compete with.

## 6. Conclusion

The "Khyshnyk" buggy, born from volunteer efforts, has proven itself as a reliable, highly mobile, and versatile vehicle for logistics, evacuation, and fire support missions near the front line. Its performance in extreme conditions—such as extracting stuck vehicles while carrying heavy loads—demonstrates its value. The ongoing production and fielding of these vehicles continue to rely on volunteer fundraising, reflecting its status as a grassroots initiative that has become indispensable to Ukrainian forces.
