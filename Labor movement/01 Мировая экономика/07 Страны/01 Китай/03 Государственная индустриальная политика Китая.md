## 3. Key Empirical Findings

**Figure 3:** Sectors with more policy interventions experienced **faster export growth** (2017–2024). The relationship is especially pronounced in motor vehicles and battery-related products.

**Figure 4:** Policy-intensive sectors saw **larger increases in trade surpluses**, consistent with both:
- Export expansion
- Import substitution (displacing foreign suppliers in domestic market)

**Magnitude:** The top 15 sectors in terms of policy support contributed **76 percent** of the increase in China's aggregate trade surplus over this period.

## 4. Interpretation and Caveats

**Why industrial policy matters for trade balances:**
- Lowers financing and production costs
- Facilitates capacity expansion and rapid scaling
- Combines export promotion with domestic import displacement
- Reinforces the economy's outward orientation by prioritizing supply (tradable sectors) over consumption (household demand, social spending)

**Caveats:**
- The analysis shows **association, not causation**
- The NIPO measure counts policy interventions equally (does not weight by magnitude)
- Industrial policy does not *automatically* lead to export success—effectiveness depends on design and implementation
- China's policies emphasize **domestic competition** among many firms rather than protecting national champions, which may enhance competitiveness
