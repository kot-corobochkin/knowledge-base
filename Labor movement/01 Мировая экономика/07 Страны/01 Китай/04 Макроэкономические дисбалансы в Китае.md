The authors note that aggregate imbalances also stem from:
- Weak domestic demand amid high household savings
- Limited social safety net and constrained consumption
- Property market downturn and limited fiscal stimulus
- Persistent deflation and a relatively weak renminbi (providing price advantages)
**Source:** François de Soyres, Ece Fisgin, Mike Liu, and Eva Van Leemput, "China’s Trade Dominance and the Role of Industrial Policies"
